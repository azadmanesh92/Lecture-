Dr. Mahsa Azadmanesh | Eng. Ali Mari Oryad | Associate Professor Dr. Krasin Georgiev | Associate Professor Dr. Ivaylo Peev

THE THREAT: The Kessler Syndrome

When Space Debris Collides with Space Debris, hundreds of smaller debris are produced and they are a threat to orbital infrastructure.

Kessler Syndrome

A self-sustaining chain reaction of collisions that renders orbits unusable.

How do we communicate this counter-intuitive physics to non-engineers?

THE CHALLENGE: Conceptual Barriers

Concepts like Energy, orbital inclination, and atmospheric drag are abstract for students.

Cognitive Load Theory
  • Traditional lectures force attention splitting between auditory definition and mental visualization.
  • This high cognitive load hampers learning in short timeframes.

THE SOLUTION: A Micro-Simulation

A short gamified intervention to offload visualization to the simulation engine.

5 Stages of Dynamics in 120 Seconds
  • Kinetic Energy: High-velocity impacts cause immediate 'Fire' state (reinforcing v^2 factor).
  • Inclination: Debris moving at 90 degrees offset maximizes collision speed.
  • Kessler Cascade: Collisions between debris pieces shatter into high-velocity shrapnel that hunts the player.
